Day 20 — Full-Scope Campaign Simulation

Goal: Conduct a multi-day, end-to-end red vs. blue simulation that incorporates reconnaissance, exploitation, privilege escalation, persistence, lateral movement, detection, hunting, and remediation — blending all skills from Days 1–19 into one continuous campaign.

Step 1 — Campaign Planning

  • Red Team:
    • Design an attack storyline that unfolds over several simulated days.
    • Include realistic dwell time between phases to mimic real adversaries.
    • Blend noisy and stealthy tactics, mixing prior techniques with new variations.
  • Blue Team:
    • Prepare monitoring infrastructure with tuned rules from prior days.
    • Establish an incident response timeline for the duration of the campaign.

Step 2 — Initial Access

  • Use one or more methods from previous exercises:
    • Phishing simulation (lab-safe payload)
    • Exploitation of a lab service
    • Compromised standard domain credentials

Step 3 — Reconnaissance and Enumeration

  • Map out lab.local using:
    • Low-noise commands and PowerShell scripts from Day 11
    • Target identification via BloodHound or manual queries

Step 4 — Privilege Escalation and Lateral Movement

  • Execute:
    • Kerberoasting or token abuse from Day 6
    • PsExec, WinRM, or WMI pivoting from Day 4
    • Movement to high-value targets (e.g., Domain Controller)

Step 5 — Persistence Deployment

  • Establish multiple persistence methods, including:
    • Registry keys, scheduled tasks, or WMI subscriptions
    • GPO modifications for domain-wide impact
    • One or more stealth persistence techniques from Day 19

Step 6 — Blue Team Threat Hunting and Response

  • Detect and disrupt:
    • Enumeration and escalation activities
    • Persistence deployments
    • Unusual logon patterns or privilege use
  • Use proactive hunts (Day 17) and persistence sweeps (Day 18)

Step 7 — Campaign Climax and Containment

  • Red team executes a final objective: Simulated data exfiltration, Ransomware-style encryption (lab-safe)
  • Blue team contains compromised systems and accounts.

Step 8 — Joint Debrief

  • Red team presents the full attack chain, including: Tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs), Timing and stealth considerations
  • Blue team presents detection points, gaps, and response metrics.
  • Collaboratively identify improvements for: Detection coverage, Incident response processes, Adversary emulation playbooks

End of Day 20 Success Criteria:

✅Complete multi-phase campaign executed without breaking lab integrity
✅Blue team detected and responded to multiple phases
✅Documented lessons learned and updated defenses for future simulations
