Day 15 — Multi-Adversary Challenge

Goal: Simulate two or more different adversary profiles in quick succession or in a blended attack chain to stress-test both red team execution and blue team detection/response under chaotic, multi-attack conditions.

Step 1 — Select Adversary Profiles

  • Choose at least two real-world groups from MITRE ATT&CK:
    • Example 1: APT29 (Cozy Bear) — stealthy espionage operations.
    • Example 2: Conti Ransomware Group — aggressive, high-speed attacks.
  • Ensure chosen groups have distinct TTPs to create variety in attack patterns.

Step 2 — Map and Combine TTPs

  • Create an attack plan that includes:
    • Initial access methods from both actors.
    • Overlapping techniques (e.g., both using PowerShell but with different goals).
    • Distinct privilege escalation or persistence methods from each.
  • Decide if attacks will be:
    • Sequential: One adversary completes their chain before the next starts.
    • Concurrent: Both attack profiles operate at the same time.

Step 3 — Red Team Execution

  • Begin with adversary A's initial access and early-stage actions.
  • Introduce adversary B's activity before adversary A completes their chain.
  • Use different tooling, command syntax, and timing to make attribution harder.
  • Record all commands, timestamps, and hostnames affected.

Step 4 — Blue Team Response

  • Monitor for:
    • Increased alert volume due to multiple attack patterns.
    • Conflicting or overlapping IOCs.
    • Changes in attacker behavior as both sets of TTPs unfold.
  • Respond in real time by prioritizing high-impact detections.

Step 5 — Stress-Test Detection Rules

  • Evaluate whether:
    • Low-noise TTPs from adversary A still bypass detection.
    • High-noise TTPs from adversary B overwhelm analysts.
    • Alerts can be correctly attributed to the right simulated adversary.

Step 6 — Debrief and Lessons Learned

  • Red team: Document blended or conflicting tactics and their impact.
  • Blue team: Identify which detections failed due to noise or confusion.
  • Jointly decide on improvements to: Event correlation, Incident prioritization, Playbook flexibility under multi-threat conditions

End of Day 15 Success Criteria:

✅Two or more adversary profiles executed in blended or overlapping fashion
✅Blue team maintained detection and response without losing situational awareness
✅Documented improvements for handling complex, multi-actor scenarios
